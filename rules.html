Rules

1. Team Composition

1.1. Budget

Each player has 80 stars to form their team.

1.2. Composition

A team must include at least one skier from each discipline (downhill, super-G, giant slalom, slalom).

A maximum of two skiers from the same nationality can be selected per discipline.

2. Point Allocation

2.1. Points After the Race

Points are awarded according to the FIS points system (1st = 100, 2nd = 80, etc.).

2.2. Race Bonuses

  • Victory: +20 points
  • Second place: +15 points
  • Third place: +10 points
  • Top 7: +5 points

2.3. Consistency Bonuses (consecutive performance series)

These bonuses apply to consecutive series of performances, but are not cumulative within the same series.

For example, a series of victories will only grant +20 points (not +20 +15 +10 +5 additional points).

  • Victory series: +40 points
  • Podium series: +30 points
  • Top 10 series (4th to 10th place): +20 points
  • Top 15 series (11th to 15th place): +10 points
  • Top 30 series (16th to 30th place): +5 points

These bonuses are added to the individual race score (e.g., a victory gives a total of 100 + 20 + 40 = 160 points).

3. Specific Bonuses

3.1. Two-Legged Races (slalom and giant slalom)

+3 points per position gained in the second leg.

Spectacular Comebacks:

  • 30th to podium: +70 points
  • 30th to Top 10: +30 points
  • 30th to 1st place: +100 points
  • Any comeback to the podium (from any position): +5 points

3.2. All Races (including two-legged races)

Red bib (discipline leader): +15 points

Best intermediate time: +2 points per intermediate time

Best run time: +5 points

3.3. Best Young Skier Ranking

Any skier under the age of 25 at the start of the season (determined by the first race in Sölden) is eligible.

Race Classification:

  • Best young skier: +5 points
  • Second best young skier: +3 points
  • Third best young skier: +1 point
  • Leader of the overall young skier ranking: +10 points per weekend

4. Restrictions and Clarifications

Ranking and consistency bonuses are cumulative.

Consistency bonuses do not combine across categories (e.g., a podium series cannot be combined with a victory series).

Performances must be validated according to the official FIS rankings to be counted.

Force majeure situations (e.g., cancellation or alteration of races) may result in adjustments to the points system.

5. Rankings and Victories

Points are totaled after each race (by 8:00 AM the next day).

Weekly, monthly, and seasonal rankings will be published.

The player with the most points at the end of the season will be crowned Fantasy Alpine Skiing Champion.

A small trophy will be awarded to the winner by the organizers.
